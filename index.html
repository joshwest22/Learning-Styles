Learning Styles

Commonalites

Carol Dweck talks about the power of having a growth mindset. She says this entails of 3 key components. Along with the list of those 3 components I have added congruent material from the Vertasium videos.

  1. Goals
    • People in a fixed mindset want to look smart at all cost, while people in a growth mindset want to learn at all costs. In the Vertasium videos there are several people who either make up something that sounds correct to them or more tellingly repeat the same answer and seem confused about the question. These people have a fixed mindset for that question they are being asked. Because their goal is to look smart rather than admit they are unsure, they wind up looking stupid precisely because the way their goal manifests is through the action of avoidance.
  2. Effort
    • People in a fixed mindset believe things should come naturally and if they don't you should avoid that thing so you don't look stupid. However, people in a growth mindset believe that you must work hard and that effort is key.
  3. Setbacks
    • People in a fixed mindset hide their mistakes and deficiencies, while those with a growth mindset capitalize on mistakes and confront deficiencies as opportunities to grow. This ties to the Veritasium videos he said that telling people the answer is not very helpful because they just reassure themselves of what they already know, essentially using a fixed mindset.
